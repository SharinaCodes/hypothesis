Nonparametric Statistical Testing

Business question

Does median annual spending vary across market regions when comparing distributions without assuming normality?

Variables

  • Dependent (numeric): annual_spend_usd
  • Independent (categorical): market_region

Testing Method

A Kruskal-Wallis test was selected because the data are right-skewed and the research question compares annual spending across three or more independent groups defined by a single categorical variable (market region). A Kruskal-Wallis test is the standard method for testing grouped, skewed data, as it ranks the data to reduce the impact of outliers.

Hypotheses

  • $H_0$: Median annual spending is equal across market regions.
  • $H_1$ : At least one market region's median annual spending is different.

ANOVA Code

In[5]:
coastal_south = df.loc[df["market_region"] == "coastal_south", "annual_spend_usd"].tolist()
inland_south = df.loc[df["market_region"] == "inland_south", "annual_spend_usd"].tolist()
inland_north = df.loc[df["market_region"] == "inland_north", "annual_spend_usd"].tolist()
coastal_north= df.loc[df["market_region"] == "coastal_north", "annual_spend_usd"].tolist()

result = stats.kruskal(coastal_south, inland_south, inland_north, coastal_north)

print("Kruskal-Wallis (annual spending by market region)")
print("statistic =", round(result.statistic, 4))
print("pvalue =", round(result.pvalue, 4))
print()

Kruskal-Wallis (annual spending by market region)
statistic = 10.3203

Results

The Kruskal-Wallis test yielded an H-statistic of 10.3203 with a p-value of 0.016. Since 0.016 < 0.05, I fail to rejecgt the null hypothesis. There is no statistically significant evidence that median annual spending differs by market region in this dataset.

Recommendations

Since median annual spending does not differ significantly by region, the organization should refrain from assuming that typical annual spending has a regional component. Instead, the organization should treat regional differences from the ANOVA as potentially driven by a small number of outliers, and focus on identifying the factors that lead to higher spending.

Limitations

While the analysis evaluates whether medians differ by market region, ti does not explain the reasons behind higher annual spending. Additionally, the analysis does not factor in other variables in the dataset.
